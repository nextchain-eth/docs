# Workshops & Learning Resources

**NextChain Hub Ethiopia | CATS 2026**

---

## Learning Approach

Learning at NextChain Hub Ethiopia was structured around **repeated exposure, local accessibility, and practical relevance**.

Rather than relying on a single hackathon moment, we designed learning as a continuous process combining:

- In-person university workshops
    
- Virtual sessions with broader reach
    
- Self-paced learning resources for beginners
    

This approach acknowledged that many participants were new to Web3 and required sustained guidance to move from interest to implementation.

---

## Virtual Sessions

To extend learning beyond physical locations, we hosted three virtual meetups:

- **Nov 20 & Nov 25** via Cardano Academy channels
    
- **Dec 15** via the NextChain Telegram community
    

Virtual sessions helped reach developers outside university settings and reinforced concepts introduced during in-person workshops.

## Local-Language Learning Resources

Recognising language as a barrier for many beginners, we intentionally shared **Amharic-language Cardano development resources**, including curated YouTube playlists from Cardano Academy.

These resources enabled:

- Self-paced learning
    
- Reinforcement of workshop content
    
- Accessibility for first-time blockchain developers

## What We Learned

This phase reinforced several insights:

- Repetition matters more than intensity
    
- Physical presence accelerates learning and collaboration
    
- Beginner-friendly resources are essential for inclusion
    
- Technical mentorship is a critical success factor
    

These learnings directly informed how we support teams and request deeper technical assistance going forward.
